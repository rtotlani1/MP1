Who is the client?
MKS Ventures is a real estate development firm based in Delhi, India. The firm operates across both residential and commercial segments — developing properties from the ground up. The client is my father, who runs the business. I have direct access to him for interviews, feedback rounds, and content approvals throughout the project.

What problem does this solve?
Right now, MKS Ventures has no dedicated web presence. Potential partners, investors, and clients have no way to learn about the firm, its portfolio, or how to get in contact — unless they already know someone at the company. A clean, professional website fixes that. It gives the firm a credible first impression and a single place to point anyone who asks "where can I learn more?"

What will the site include?
- A single-page static website with the following sections:
- A header with the firm name and a short tagline
- An about section covering the firm's background and focus areas
- A projects section showcasing past and ongoing developments (residential +     commercial)
- A contact section with basic information for reaching the firm

Who is the audience?
Primarily potential investors, business partners, and high-intent buyers or tenants — people who already have some context and are looking to verify credibility and get in touch.
